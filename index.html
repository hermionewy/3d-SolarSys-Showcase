About 4.6 billion years ago, the Solar System formed and since became home to the sun, and eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune.

The Sun is enormous -- about 10,000 times larger than Earth in volume and weighs about 333,000 times as much as Earth. Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only planet in the Universe known to harbor life. Please take really good care of it as its our only home.

Most of Earth's surface is covered with water which bestows it the charming blue, with plants adding green, and glaciers embellishing white. But only a tiny portion of the water is drinkable.

You shall never feel along in this world -- about 7.6 billion humans live on this blue planet. Politically, the world has about 200 countries and each has its own culture. It is important to embrace divesity and celebrate differences. Learning from each other's merits makes the world a better place.

Education is the key to everything that is good in our world today. Higher literacy levels help to enrich an individual's life as well as enhance a country's economy.
